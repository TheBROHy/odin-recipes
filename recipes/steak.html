Marinated Flank Steak

Flank steak is a flavorful cut of beef that is perfect for grilling or broiling. Marinating it enhances its tenderness and flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds flank steak
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, olive oil, red wine vinegar, garlic, black pepper, rosemary, thyme, salt, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and lemon juice.
  2. Place the flank steak in a resealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over it. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Remove the steak from the marinade and discard the marinade.
  5. Grill the steak for about 5-7 minutes per side for medium-rare doneness.
  6. Let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing against the grain.
  7. Serve with your favorite sides.

Notes

  • For best results, marinate the steak overnight.
  • Adjust the cooking time based on your desired level of doneness.
  • Serve with grilled vegetables or a fresh salad.
  • Leftover steak can be sliced and used in salads, sandwiches, or tacos.
  • Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • Reheat in a skillet or microwave before serving.
  • For a spicy kick, add red pepper flakes to the marinade.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices in the marinade for unique flavors.
  • Flank steak is best cooked to medium-rare for optimal tenderness.
